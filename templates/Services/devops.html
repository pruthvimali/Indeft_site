Containerization

Containerization is a subset of virtualization where abstracts of software run in isolated spaces called containers, while employing the shared operating system. These containers include everything an application needs to run- libraries, files, dependencies, binaries, etc., all encapsulated together. As such environments are lightweight and self-sufficient, it decreases business costs by a significant margin.
